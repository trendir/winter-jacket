Discovering The Unique Food Scene In Indiana A Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 31, 2023 · 5 min read

Indiana is known for its corn fields and basketball, but did you know that it has a unique food scene as well? Whether you're a foodie or just looking for something different to try, Indiana has plenty of options for you to explore.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to unique food in Indiana is knowing where to go and what to try. With so many options, it can be overwhelming to narrow down your choices. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this guide to the best unique food in Indiana.

Are you looking for classic Midwest dishes with a twist? Or maybe you want to try international cuisine with a local flare? Whatever your taste buds desire, Indiana has something for everyone.

In this article, we'll explore some of the best unique food options in Indiana and provide you with all the information you need to plan your next food adventure. From regional specialties to trendy new restaurants, Indiana has it all.

The Famous Breaded Pork Tenderloin Sandwich

When it comes to unique food in Indiana, one dish stands out above the rest: the breaded pork tenderloin sandwich. This classic Hoosier dish is a must-try for anyone visiting the state.

One of the best places to try this sandwich is at Nick's Kitchen in Huntington, Indiana. Nick's has been serving up their famous breaded pork tenderloin sandwich since 1904 and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike. The sandwich is made with a tender pork loin that is pounded thin, breaded, and deep-fried to perfection. It's then served on a soft bun with your choice of toppings.

Fried Chicken and Waffles

Another classic dish with a local twist is fried chicken and waffles. While this dish is not unique to Indiana, the Hoosier state has put its own spin on it.

One place to try this dish is at Maxine's Chicken and Waffles in Indianapolis. Their version of this classic dish is made with a crispy fried chicken breast and a fluffy Belgian waffle. It's then drizzled with maple syrup and served with a side of butter. It's the perfect combination of savory and sweet.

Sugar Cream Pie

Sugar cream pie is a regional specialty that can only be found in Indiana. This rich and creamy pie is made with a custard filling that is flavored with vanilla and nutmeg. It's then topped with a sprinkling of cinnamon sugar and baked to golden perfection.

If you're looking to try this classic Hoosier dessert, head to Wick's Pies in Winchester, Indiana. They've been making sugar cream pies since 1944 and are known for their delicious pies made with quality ingredients.

How to Make Indiana-Style Chili

Indiana-style chili is a unique twist on the classic dish. It's made with ground beef, kidney beans, and a blend of spices, including cinnamon and cloves. It's then served over spaghetti noodles and topped with cheese and onions.

If you want to try making this dish at home, here's a simple recipe to get you started:

-1 pound ground beef

-1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed

-1 can diced tomatoes

-1 tablespoon chili powder

-1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

-1/2 teaspoon ground cloves

-Salt and pepper to taste

-Cooked spaghetti noodles

-Shredded cheddar cheese and diced onions for topping

1. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Drain any excess fat.

2. Add the kidney beans, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cinnamon, and cloves to the skillet. Stir to combine.

3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

4. Serve the chili over cooked spaghetti noodles and top with shredded cheddar cheese and diced onions.

Culinary Tours

If you want to experience the best of Indiana's unique food scene, consider taking a culinary tour. These tours will take you to some of the best restaurants and food shops in the state and give you a chance to sample a variety of dishes.

One company that offers culinary tours in Indiana is Taste Indy Food Tours. They offer several different tours, including a downtown food tour and a beer and food pairing tour. Each tour includes multiple stops and plenty of food to sample.
