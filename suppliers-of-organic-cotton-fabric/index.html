Suppliers Of Organic Cotton Fabric Where To Find Them

Written by Eveline Jun 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for suppliers of organic cotton fabric for your sustainable fashion brand, but don't know where to start? It can be overwhelming to navigate the world of textile sourcing, especially when trying to prioritize ethical and eco-friendly options. But fear not, we've got you covered.

As a fashion industry insider, you're likely aware of the pain points that come with finding reliable suppliers of organic cotton fabric. You want to ensure that the cotton is truly organic, that the workers who produced it were treated fairly, and that the fabric is of high quality. It can be challenging to find all of these elements in one supplier, but it is possible.

First and foremost, it's essential to have a clear understanding of your target audience and what they value. Are they willing to pay a premium for sustainable fabrics? Do they prioritize transparency and ethical production practices? Understanding your target can help you make informed decisions when it comes to sourcing materials.

When it comes to suppliers of organic cotton fabric, there are a few key players in the market. Some of the most well-known include:

1. Organic Cotton Plus

Organic Cotton Plus is a US-based supplier that offers a wide variety of organic cotton fabrics, including twills, voiles, and jersey. They prioritize transparency and sustainability, with a commitment to using non-GMO cotton and supporting small-scale farmers. They also offer a range of certifications, including GOTS and Fair Trade.

2. Offset Warehouse

Offset Warehouse is a UK-based supplier that focuses on eco-friendly and sustainable textiles. Their organic cotton offerings include a range of prints and patterns, as well as solid colors. They prioritize transparency and ethical production practices, and offer certifications such as GOTS and Soil Association.

3. Simplifi Fabric

Simplifi Fabric is a Canadian supplier that offers a variety of organic cotton fabrics, including knits, wovens, and fleece. They prioritize sustainability and transparency, with a commitment to using low-impact dyes and supporting small-scale farmers. They offer certifications such as GOTS and Oeko-Tex.

While these are just a few examples, there are many other suppliers of organic cotton fabric available. It's important to do your research and find a supplier that aligns with your values and priorities.

Why I Choose Organic Cotton Plus

As a sustainable fashion designer, I prioritize transparency and ethical production practices in all aspects of my business. That's why I choose to work with Organic Cotton Plus for my organic cotton fabric needs. Not only do they offer a wide variety of fabric options, but they also prioritize sustainability and fair labor practices. Plus, their certifications give me peace of mind knowing that the fabric I'm using is truly organic and eco-friendly.

How to Choose the Right Supplier for You

When it comes to choosing a supplier of organic cotton fabric, it's important to consider a few key factors:

1. Certifications

Look for suppliers that offer certifications such as GOTS, Fair Trade, or Oeko-Tex. These certifications ensure that the cotton is truly organic and that the production process is transparent and ethical.

2. Sustainability

Consider the supplier's commitment to sustainability. Do they use low-impact dyes? Do they support small-scale farmers? These factors can make a big difference in the overall eco-friendliness of the fabric.

3. Quality and Variety

Make sure the supplier offers a wide variety of fabric options and that the quality is up to par. You don't want to sacrifice quality for sustainability.

My Experience with Simplifi Fabric

While I primarily work with Organic Cotton Plus for my organic cotton fabric needs, I have also had positive experiences with Simplifi Fabric. Their commitment to sustainability and transparency is impressive, and their fabric options are high-quality. Plus, their Canadian location makes them a great option for North American-based designers.

Question and Answer

Q: Are there any other certifications I should look for when choosing a supplier of organic cotton fabric?

A: In addition to GOTS, Fair Trade, and Oeko-Tex, you may also want to look for certifications such as USDA Organic and Global Recycled Standard (GRS).

Q: Can I request a sample of fabric from a supplier before making a purchase?

A: Yes, most suppliers will offer swatches or small samples for a small fee or for free.

Q: Is organic cotton more expensive than conventional cotton?

A: Yes, organic cotton is generally more expensive due to the higher cost of production and labor. However, many sustainable fashion brands are willing to pay a premium for eco-friendly fabrics.

Q: Can I visit the supplier's facilities to see their production processes firsthand?

A: It depends on the supplier. Some may allow visits, while others may not. Contact the supplier directly to find out.

Conclusion of Suppliers of Organic Cotton Fabric

When it comes to finding suppliers of organic cotton fabric, there are many options available. It's important to prioritize transparency, sustainability, and quality when making your decision. By doing your research and choosing a supplier that aligns with your values, you can feel confident in the fabrics you're using for your sustainable fashion brand.
