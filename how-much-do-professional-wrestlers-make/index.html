How Much Do Professional Wrestlers Make

Written by Aprilia Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much professional wrestlers make? It's a common question that many people have. Professional wrestling is a popular sport that has a large following, but not many people know how much the wrestlers make. If you're curious about how much money professional wrestlers make, keep reading.

Professional wrestlers often face many pain points when it comes to their salary. They may work long hours, endure physical injuries, and have to travel frequently. Despite these challenges, many wrestlers love what they do and are willing to make sacrifices to pursue their passion.

The amount of money that professional wrestlers make can vary greatly depending on their level of experience, popularity, and success. According to Forbes, the top earning wrestler in 2020 was Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson, who made $87.5 million. However, the average wrestler makes significantly less than that.

In general, entry-level wrestlers can expect to make around $25,000 per year. However, as they gain more experience and become more popular, their salary can increase significantly. Mid-level wrestlers can make anywhere from $40,000 to $150,000 per year, while top-level wrestlers can make millions of dollars.

To summarize, the amount of money professional wrestlers make can vary greatly depending on their level of experience, popularity, and success. Entry-level wrestlers can expect to make around $25,000 per year, while top-level wrestlers can make millions of dollars.

How Much Do WWE Wrestlers Make?

As one of the biggest wrestling organizations in the world, WWE is known for offering its wrestlers lucrative contracts. WWE wrestlers typically make more money than wrestlers in other organizations. However, their salary can still vary greatly depending on their level of success.

As of 2021, the minimum salary for a WWE wrestler is $80,000. However, top-level wrestlers can make much more than that. According to Forbes, WWE wrestler Brock Lesnar made $10 million in 2020. Other top-earning WWE wrestlers include John Cena, Roman Reigns, and Randy Orton.

How Do Independent Wrestlers Make Money?

Independent wrestlers, also known as "indie" wrestlers, do not have the backing of a major wrestling organization like WWE. As a result, they may have to work harder to make a living from wrestling.

Independent wrestlers typically make money through a combination of wrestling matches, merchandise sales, and appearance fees. They may also work a day job to supplement their income. While indie wrestlers may not make as much money as WWE wrestlers, they have more creative freedom and can build a loyal fanbase.

Factors That Affect How Much Professional Wrestlers Make

Several factors can affect how much money professional wrestlers make. These include:

Experience: Entry-level wrestlers typically make less money than wrestlers with more experience.

Popularity: The more popular a wrestler is, the more money they can make from merchandise sales and appearance fees.

Success: Successful wrestlers can command higher salaries and earn bonuses for winning matches and titles.

How to Become a Professional Wrestler

If you're interested in becoming a professional wrestler, there are several steps you can take:

Train: Attend a wrestling school to learn how to wrestle and perform stunts safely.

Get Experience: Participate in local wrestling events to gain experience and build your reputation.

Network: Attend wrestling conventions and events to meet other wrestlers and industry insiders.

Promote Yourself: Use social media to promote yourself and build a following.

Conclusion of How Much Do Professional Wrestlers Make

Professional wrestlers can make a wide range of salaries depending on their level of experience, popularity, and success. Entry-level wrestlers can expect to make around $25,000 per year, while top-level wrestlers can make millions of dollars. WWE wrestlers typically make more money than indie wrestlers, but indie wrestlers have more creative freedom. If you're interested in becoming a professional wrestler, it's important to train, gain experience, network, and promote yourself.
