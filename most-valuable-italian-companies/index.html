Eni

Eni is an Italian multinational oil and gas company headquartered in Rome. It is one of the largest companies in the world and has a significant presence in more than 70 countries. Eni is involved in every aspect of the oil and gas industry, from exploration and production to refining and marketing. The company has a diverse portfolio of assets, including oil and gas reserves, refineries, and petrochemical plants. Eni's success is due in part to its focus on innovation and sustainability. The company has invested heavily in renewable energy, and it is committed to reducing its carbon footprint.

Eni's stock is traded on the Milan Stock Exchange, and it has a market capitalization of over 40 billion euros.

Ferrari

Ferrari is a luxury sports car manufacturer based in Maranello, Italy. The company was founded in 1947 by Enzo Ferrari and has since become one of the most iconic brands in the world. Ferrari is known for its high-performance sports cars, which are designed and manufactured with the utmost attention to detail. The company also has a successful racing division, which has won numerous championships over the years.

Ferrari's stock is traded on the New York Stock Exchange and the Milan Stock Exchange, and it has a market capitalization of over 30 billion euros.

UniCredit

UniCredit is one of the largest banks in Italy and has a significant presence throughout Europe. The company offers a wide range of financial products and services, including retail banking, corporate banking, and investment banking. UniCredit has a strong focus on technology and innovation, and it has invested heavily in digital banking solutions. The company is also committed to sustainability and has set ambitious targets for reducing its carbon footprint.

UniCredit's stock is traded on the Milan Stock Exchange, and it has a market capitalization of over 20 billion euros.
