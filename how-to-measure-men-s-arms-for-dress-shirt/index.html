How to Measure Men's Arms for Dress Shirt: A Step-by-Step Guide

When it comes to buying dress shirts, one of the most important measurements to consider is arm length. But don't worry, we're here to help! In this post, we'll walk you through the steps of measuring your arms for a dress shirt, so you can find the perfect fit.

To measure your arms for a dress shirt, you'll need a measuring tape and a friend to help you. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Find Your Starting Point

Start by standing up straight with your arms at your sides. Your friend should measure from the center of your back, just below the base of your neck, to the point where you want your shirt sleeves to end (usually at the wrist bone).

Step 2: Measure Your Arm Length

Next, extend your arm straight out to the side, parallel to the floor. Your friend should measure from the center of your back to the bone at the top of your shoulder (the acromion). Then, they should measure from the acromion to the point where you want your shirt sleeves to end (usually at the wrist bone).

Step 3: Compare Your Measurements

Once you have both measurements, compare them to find your arm length. This is the measurement you'll use when shopping for dress shirts.

Step 4: Check the Fit

When trying on dress shirts, make sure the sleeves end at the point where you want them to (usually at the wrist bone). The cuffs should be snug but not tight, and you should be able to move your arms comfortably.

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when measuring your arms for a dress shirt:

Tip #2: Have a Friend Help You

Q: Can I measure my arms by myself?
