1920 Male Dress Code A Guide To Gatsby Style In Relaxed English

Written by Oliver Aug 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you attending a 1920s-themed party or event and feeling lost about what to wear? Do you want to channel the dapper style of Jay Gatsby and his peers? The 1920 male dress code can be intimidating, but fear not! In this guide, we'll break down the basics and help you put together a stylish and authentic 1920s outfit.

The 1920s was a time of significant change in fashion, with men's clothing becoming more relaxed and comfortable. However, certain dress codes still applied, and it can be challenging to navigate them when trying to create an authentic 1920s look. Additionally, some clothing items that were popular in the 1920s may not be practical or comfortable for modern-day wear.

The target of the 1920 male dress code is to create an outfit that combines both the formal and informal elements of 1920s fashion, resulting in a stylish yet comfortable look. This dress code aims to recreate the dapper and suave style of the era, making it a popular choice for events such as weddings, parties, and even Halloween.

In summary, the 1920 male dress code is all about combining formal and informal elements to create a stylish and authentic look that channels the suave style of Jay Gatsby and his peers.

The Roaring Twenties: A Personal Experience

As someone who has attended several 1920s-themed parties and events, I can attest to the fun and excitement of dressing up in Gatsby style. There's something about the era's fashion that exudes confidence and elegance. However, it can be challenging to put together an outfit that is both stylish and authentic.

One of the key elements of the 1920 male dress code is the suit. A well-tailored suit was a staple of 1920s fashion, and it's essential to choose the right one for your body type. Slim-fit suits with high-waisted trousers and wide lapels were popular during the time, and they're making a comeback today.

The Importance of Accessories

Accessories were a crucial part of 1920s fashion, and they're an easy way to add a touch of authenticity to your outfit. A fedora or newsboy cap is a must-have accessory for any 1920s-inspired outfit. A silk scarf or tie can add a pop of color to a neutral suit, while suspenders can be a stylish alternative to a belt.

The Role of Shoes

Shoes are another essential element of the 1920 male dress code. Oxfords and brogues were popular choices during the era and are still stylish today. Look for shoes with a round or pointed toe and a low heel for a more authentic look.

How to Tie a Bow Tie

A bow tie is a classic accessory that can add a touch of sophistication to any outfit. However, tying one can be challenging, especially if you've never done it before. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to tie a bow tie:

1. Start with the bow tie around your neck, with one end hanging about two inches lower than the other.

2. Cross the longer end over the shorter end and bring it up and over the bow.

3. Pinch the bow in the center and bring the longer end down over the center of the bow.

4. Fold the shorter end in half and pinch it against the longer end.

5. Bring the longer end back up over the center of the bow and tuck it through the loop at the back.

6. Adjust the bow and straighten the ends as needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear a vest instead of a suit jacket?

A: Yes, a vest can be a stylish alternative to a suit jacket and was a popular choice during the 1920s. However, make sure it fits well and matches the rest of your outfit.

Q: Can I wear sneakers with my 1920s outfit?

A: While sneakers are comfortable and practical, they may not be the best choice for a 1920s-inspired outfit. Look for dress shoes or loafers instead.

Q: Can I wear suspenders and a belt?

A: No, suspenders and belts should not be worn together. Choose one or the other.

Q: Can I wear a pocket square with my suit?

A: Yes, a pocket square can add a touch of sophistication to a suit. Choose a silk or cotton square in a complementary color to your outfit.

Conclusion of 1920 Male Dress Code

The 1920 male dress code is all about combining formal and informal elements to create a stylish and authentic look that channels the suave style of Jay Gatsby and his peers. From well-tailored suits to stylish accessories, the 1920s had a unique fashion sense that still influences us today. By following the tips in this guide, you'll be able to put together a dapper and authentic 1920s-inspired outfit that will turn heads at any event.
