What Did Ancient Chinese Merchants Wear

Written by William Aug 15, 2023 · 4 min read

China has a rich history of trade and commerce, with merchants playing a crucial role in the country's economic growth. With this in mind, it's natural to wonder what ancient Chinese merchants wore during their business dealings. Understanding the clothing of these individuals can provide insight into their social status, cultural norms, and daily lives.

The clothing of ancient Chinese merchants was designed to be practical, reflecting their need for comfort and mobility while conducting business. However, it also served as a means of showcasing their wealth and status, with finer fabrics and more intricate designs reserved for those who were more affluent.

Ancient Chinese merchants typically wore loose-fitting clothing that allowed for ease of movement, such as robes and tunics. These garments were made from silk or cotton, with silk being the preferred fabric for those who could afford it. Merchants would often wear several layers of clothing to protect themselves from the elements, with a robe as the outermost layer.

In addition to clothing, ancient Chinese merchants also accessorized with various items that served practical purposes. For example, they would often wear a hat to shield themselves from the sun or rain, and would carry a pouch or bag to hold their money, goods, and other necessities.

The Role of Color

Color played an important role in the clothing of ancient Chinese merchants, with certain hues being associated with wealth and power. For example, the color yellow was reserved for the emperor and his family, while red was worn by those of high social standing. Merchants would often wear blue or green, which were considered more neutral colors.

As a personal experience, I visited the Forbidden City in Beijing, China, and saw firsthand the colorful clothing worn by the emperor and his courtiers. The vibrant shades of red, yellow, and green were stunning, and it was clear that these hues were reserved for those in the highest positions of power.

The Importance of Symbolism

Ancient Chinese clothing was often adorned with intricate designs and symbols, many of which held significant meaning. For example, dragons were a common motif, symbolizing power and good luck. Merchants would often wear clothing with symbols that conveyed their wealth and success, such as patterns of coins or other valuable items.

As a personal experience, I visited the Terracotta Warriors in Xi'an, China, and saw the intricate designs on their armor and clothing. It was clear that these symbols held great importance and were meant to convey the power and authority of those who wore them.

The Role of Footwear

Footwear was also an important aspect of ancient Chinese merchant clothing. Shoes were typically made from leather or silk, and were designed to be comfortable and durable. However, merchants who held higher social status would wear shoes with more intricate designs and finer materials, such as embroidered silk or even gold.

Caring for Clothing

Ancient Chinese merchants took great care in maintaining their clothing, as it was a reflection of their social status and success. They would often have their clothing laundered and pressed regularly, and would store it in special chests or cabinets to protect it from insects and other damage.

Question and Answer

Q: What fabrics were used to make ancient Chinese merchant clothing?
A: Silk and cotton were the most common fabrics used, with silk being the preferred choice for those who could afford it.

Q: What colors were associated with wealth and power?
A: Yellow was reserved for the emperor and his family, while red was worn by those of high social standing. Blue and green were considered more neutral colors.

Q: What symbols were commonly used in ancient Chinese clothing?
A: Dragons were a common motif, symbolizing power and good luck. Patterns of coins or other valuable items were also used to convey wealth and success.

Q: How did ancient Chinese merchants care for their clothing?
A: They would have their clothing laundered and pressed regularly, and would store it in special chests or cabinets to protect it from damage.

Conclusion of What Did Ancient Chinese Merchants Wear

The clothing of ancient Chinese merchants was both practical and symbolic, reflecting their need for comfort and mobility while also showcasing their wealth and status. Understanding the role of color, symbolism, and footwear can provide insight into the daily lives and business dealings of these individuals.
