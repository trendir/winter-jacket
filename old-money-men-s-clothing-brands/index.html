Old Money Men s Clothing Brands A Guide To Timeless Style

Written by William Sep 07, 2023 · 5 min read

Do you ever feel like modern fashion just doesn't cut it? Maybe you're looking for something with a bit more class and sophistication. That's where old money men's clothing brands come in. These brands have been around for decades, if not centuries, and offer timeless styles that never go out of fashion. But with so many options out there, how do you know which ones to choose?

When it comes to old money men's clothing brands, the pain points are clear. Many men today feel like fashion has become too fast-paced and disposable. They want clothing that will last for years, if not decades, and that will never go out of style. They also want clothing that reflects their status and success, without being too flashy or ostentatious.

The target of old money men's clothing brands is clear: men who value quality, tradition, and timeless style. These brands offer clothing that is made to last, with attention to detail and craftsmanship that is hard to find in modern fashion. They also offer classic styles that never go out of fashion, from tailored suits to preppy sweaters and everything in between.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at some of the top old money men's clothing brands and what makes them so special. We'll also explore some of the key elements of old money style, from fabrics to colors to accessories, and how you can incorporate these into your own wardrobe.

Brooks Brothers: The Quintessential Old Money Brand

When it comes to old money men's clothing brands, Brooks Brothers is perhaps the most iconic. Founded in 1818, this brand has been dressing men of distinction for over 200 years. Their classic styles and attention to detail have made them a favorite of everyone from presidents to movie stars.

Personally, I love Brooks Brothers because of their commitment to quality. Their suits are made from the finest fabrics, with impeccable tailoring that ensures a perfect fit. They also offer a wide range of classic styles, from preppy chinos and polo shirts to elegant sport coats and dress shoes.

J. Press: The Ivy League Look

Another classic old money men's clothing brand is J. Press. Founded in 1902, this brand has been synonymous with the Ivy League look for generations. Their signature styles include khaki pants, button-down shirts, and tweed jackets, all in classic colors like navy and olive.

Personally, I love J. Press for their preppy style. Their clothing is perfect for a day on the golf course or a weekend in the Hamptons. They also offer a range of accessories, from ties to cufflinks, that add the perfect finishing touch to any outfit.

Fabrics and Colors of Old Money Style

One of the key elements of old money style is the use of high-quality fabrics. Wool, cashmere, and silk are all popular choices, as they are soft, durable, and have a timeless look and feel. When it comes to colors, classic shades like navy, gray, and beige are always in style, as are bold patterns like stripes and plaids.

Personally, I love wearing tweed jackets in the fall and winter. They give off a classic, old-school vibe that is hard to beat. I also love wearing chinos in the summer, paired with a crisp white polo shirt and loafers.

How to Incorporate Old Money Style into Your Wardrobe

If you're looking to incorporate old money style into your wardrobe, there are a few key pieces to consider. A tailored suit is always a good choice, as it will never go out of fashion. Look for classic styles in neutral colors like navy or gray. Pair your suit with a crisp white dress shirt and a solid tie for a timeless look.

You should also invest in a few high-quality sport coats and blazers. These can be worn with everything from dress pants to jeans, and will instantly elevate your look. Look for styles in classic fabrics like tweed or herringbone, and in neutral colors like navy or brown.

Finally, don't forget about accessories. A classic leather belt, a pair of loafers, and a silk tie are all must-haves for any old money wardrobe. These pieces will add the perfect finishing touch to any outfit.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between old money style and preppy style?

A: While there is some overlap between the two, old money style is generally more formal and traditional than preppy style. Old money style is all about classic tailoring and high-quality fabrics, while preppy style is more about bright colors and bold patterns.

Q: Can I wear old money style if I'm not wealthy?

A: Absolutely! While some old money men's clothing brands can be expensive, there are plenty of affordable options out there. Look for classic styles at stores like J.Crew or Banana Republic, or shop vintage to find high-quality pieces at a fraction of the cost.

Q: What should I wear to a job interview in an old money industry?

A: When it comes to job interviews in old money industries like finance or law, it's always best to err on the side of formality. A tailored suit in navy or gray is always a good choice, paired with a white dress shirt and a conservative tie. Avoid anything too flashy or trendy, as you want to project an image of professionalism and competence.

Q: Can I wear old money style if I'm young?

A: Absolutely! Old money style is all about timeless elegance, and can be worn by men of all ages. Whether you're a young professional just starting out or a seasoned executive, there's a place for old money style in your wardrobe.

Conclusion of Old Money Men's Clothing Brands

Old money men's clothing brands offer timeless styles that never go out of fashion. From Brooks Brothers to J. Press, these brands have been dressing men of distinction for decades, if not centuries. To incorporate old money style into your own wardrobe, look for high-quality fabrics, classic colors, and traditional tailoring. Invest in a few key pieces, like a tailored suit or a tweed jacket, and accessorize with items like a leather belt or a silk tie. With the right pieces, you can achieve a timeless look that will never go out of style.
