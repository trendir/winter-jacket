Vintage Sunglass Brands A Guide To The Best

Written by Bardi Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you love the look of vintage sunglasses, but don't know where to start? The world of vintage eyewear can be overwhelming, but fear not! In this guide, we'll walk you through the best vintage sunglass brands and what makes them special.

When it comes to vintage sunglasses, there are a few pain points to consider. First, finding a pair that fits your face properly can be a challenge. Second, many vintage frames are delicate and require careful handling. And finally, the cost of vintage eyewear can be high.

So, what's the target of vintage sunglass brands? Essentially, these brands specialize in creating new eyewear designs that are inspired by vintage styles. This means you can get the look of vintage sunglasses without the hassle of finding a well-preserved pair or worrying about delicate frames.

In this article, we'll cover the best vintage sunglass brands and what makes them stand out. From classic Ray-Bans to lesser-known labels, we'll give you the rundown on the most stylish and iconic vintage eyewear styles.

Ray-Ban: The Classic Choice

When it comes to vintage sunglasses, Ray-Ban is a brand that needs no introduction. Founded in 1937, Ray-Ban has been a staple of American eyewear for decades. Their iconic Wayfarer and Aviator styles have been worn by everyone from James Dean to Tom Cruise.

Personally, I love my vintage Ray-Ban Wayfarers. They fit perfectly and add a touch of classic cool to any outfit. Plus, they're surprisingly durable for a vintage frame.

Oliver Peoples: The Luxury Choice

If you're looking for a more upscale take on vintage eyewear, Oliver Peoples is the brand for you. Founded in 1987, this Los Angeles-based brand is known for their high-end frames and attention to detail.

I had the pleasure of trying on some Oliver Peoples sunglasses at a boutique in NYC, and I was blown away by the quality. The frames were sturdy yet lightweight, and the lenses were crystal clear. If you're willing to splurge on a pair of sunglasses, Oliver Peoples is the way to go.

Warby Parker: The Affordable Choice

For those on a budget, Warby Parker offers a range of vintage-inspired frames at a reasonable price point. Founded in 2010, Warby Parker has quickly become a go-to for stylish and affordable eyewear.

I own a few pairs of Warby Parker sunglasses, and I'm always impressed by how well-made they are for the price. Plus, their virtual try-on feature makes it easy to find a pair that fits your face perfectly.

How to Care for Vintage Sunglasses

If you do decide to invest in a pair of vintage sunglasses, it's important to take proper care of them. Here are a few tips:

  • Store them in a protective case to prevent scratches and damage.
  • Avoid exposing them to extreme temperatures or moisture.
  • Clean them with a microfiber cloth and gentle cleaning solution.
  • If they need repairs, take them to a professional eyewear repair shop.

Conclusion of Vintage Sunglass Brands

Whether you're a fan of classic Ray-Bans or prefer a more luxurious frame, there's a vintage sunglass brand out there for you. Just remember to take care of your frames and enjoy the timeless style they bring to any outfit.

Question and Answer

Q: Are vintage sunglasses more expensive than new sunglasses?

A: It depends on the brand and style. Some vintage frames can be quite expensive due to their rarity and historical value. However, many vintage-inspired brands offer affordable options that mimic the look of vintage frames.

Q: Can you still get prescription lenses in vintage frames?

A: Yes, many eyewear shops offer prescription lenses for vintage frames. However, it's important to note that not all frames are suitable for prescription lenses, so be sure to check with your optometrist before making a purchase.

Q: What are some other vintage sunglass brands worth checking out?

A: Other notable vintage sunglass brands include Persol, Alain Mikli, and Carrera. Each brand has its own unique style and history, so it's worth doing some research to find the perfect pair for you.

Q: Can I sell my vintage sunglasses?

A: Yes, vintage sunglasses can be quite valuable, especially if they're in good condition and from a well-known brand. You can sell them online or at a vintage eyewear shop.
