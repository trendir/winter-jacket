How To Buy A Suit Jacket A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you in need of a new suit jacket? Whether it's for a job interview, a wedding, or another formal event, buying a suit jacket can be a daunting task. With so many styles, fabrics, and colors to choose from, it's easy to feel overwhelmed. But fear not, because we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to buy a suit jacket.

When it comes to buying a suit jacket, the process can be overwhelming. You may not know where to start and what to look for. You may be worried about finding the right fit or choosing the right fabric. These are all common concerns that many people face when buying a suit jacket.

Understanding Your Needs

The first step in buying a suit jacket is understanding your needs. Consider the occasion for which you will be wearing the jacket. Is it for a job interview, a wedding, or another formal event? This will help you determine the appropriate style and color of the jacket.

You should also consider your body type and the fit of the jacket. Do you prefer a slim fit or a regular fit? Do you need a jacket with extra room in the shoulders or chest? Knowing your body type and the fit you prefer will help you choose the right size and style of jacket.

Another important factor to consider is the fabric of the jacket. The most common fabrics for suit jackets are wool, cotton, and linen. Wool is a popular choice for its durability and versatility, while cotton is a good option for warmer climates. Linen is lightweight and breathable, making it a great choice for summer events.

Get Measured

The next step in buying a suit jacket is getting measured. It's important to know your exact measurements to ensure a proper fit. This includes your chest, waist, and sleeve length. Most department stores and men's clothing stores offer free measuring services.

When getting measured, make sure to wear a shirt that fits well and is similar in style to the shirt you will be wearing with your suit jacket. This will ensure a more accurate measurement.

Choosing the Right Style

Once you have a good understanding of your needs and have been measured, it's time to choose the right style of suit jacket. There are many different styles to choose from, including single-breasted, double-breasted, and three-button jackets.

Single-breasted jackets are the most common style and are suitable for most occasions. Double-breasted jackets are more formal and are typically worn for black-tie events. Three-button jackets are a more modern style and are suitable for less formal occasions.

Color and Pattern

When choosing the color and pattern of your suit jacket, it's important to consider the occasion for which you will be wearing it. For formal events, a black or navy jacket is a safe choice. For less formal occasions, you can choose from a wider range of colors and patterns.

It's important to choose a color and pattern that complements your skin tone and personal style. If you're unsure, stick with a classic color like navy or charcoal and a subtle pattern like pinstripes.

Try It On

Once you've chosen a suit jacket that meets your needs and preferences, it's time to try it on. Make sure to try on the jacket with a dress shirt and dress shoes to get the full effect.

When trying on the jacket, pay attention to the fit. The jacket should fit comfortably around the shoulders, chest, and waist. The sleeves should end at your wrist bone, and the length of the jacket should cover your rear end.

Question and Answer

Q: How should a suit jacket fit?

A: A suit jacket should fit comfortably around the shoulders, chest, and waist. The sleeves should end at your wrist bone, and the length of the jacket should cover your rear end.

Q: What color suit jacket should I wear to a job interview?

A: A black or navy suit jacket is a safe choice for a job interview.

Q: What's the difference between a single-breasted and double-breasted suit jacket?

A: Single-breasted jackets have one column of buttons down the front, while double-breasted jackets have two columns of buttons.

Q: How do I care for my suit jacket?

A: Always follow the care instructions on the label of your suit jacket. Generally, you should have it dry cleaned every few wears and store it on a hanger in a breathable garment bag.

Conclusion

Buying a suit jacket doesn't have to be a stressful experience. By understanding your needs, getting measured, and choosing the right style, color, and pattern, you can find the perfect suit jacket for any occasion. Remember to try on the jacket before purchasing and take care of it properly to ensure it lasts for years to come.
