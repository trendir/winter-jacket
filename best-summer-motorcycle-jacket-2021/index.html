What to Look for When Choosing a Summer Motorcycle Jacket

When choosing a summer motorcycle jacket, there are several key factors to consider:

1. Material

The material of the jacket is essential for both protection and comfort. Look for jackets made from durable, abrasion-resistant materials like mesh, Cordura, or leather. These materials offer excellent protection in case of an accident and are also breathable enough to keep you cool and comfortable on hot days.

2. Armor

Make sure the jacket has CE-certified armor in the shoulders and elbows, and look for a pocket for a back protector. Armor is crucial for protecting your body in case of an accident, so it's essential to choose a jacket that offers high-quality, reliable protection.

3. Fit and Comfort

The fit and comfort of the jacket are also essential factors to consider. Look for jackets with a tailored or relaxed fit that feel comfortable and don't restrict your movement. Make sure the jacket has adjustable features like cuffs, waistbands, and collars to ensure a snug, comfortable fit.

4. Style

The style of the jacket is a matter of personal preference, but it's still an important factor to consider. Look for jackets that match your style and personality and that you feel comfortable and confident wearing. Remember, you'll be wearing this jacket for hours on end, so it's essential to choose one that you love.

How to Care for Your Summer Motorcycle Jacket

Caring for your summer motorcycle jacket is essential to ensure it lasts as long as possible and continues to provide reliable protection. Here are a few tips:

1. Clean it regularly

Wash your jacket regularly to remove dirt and grime that can damage the fabric. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and drying.

2. Store it properly

When you're not wearing your jacket, store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Hang it up on a hanger or hook to help it keep its shape.

3. Treat it with care

Avoid exposing your jacket to harsh chemicals or abrasive surfaces, which can damage the fabric. Be gentle when putting it on and taking it off, and don't pull or stretch the material.
