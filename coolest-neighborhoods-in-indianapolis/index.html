1. Fountain Square

Fountain Square is quickly becoming one of the trendiest neighborhoods in Indianapolis. It's known for its artsy vibe, with a variety of galleries, theaters, and music venues. The neighborhood also boasts some of the best restaurants and bars in Indianapolis, making it a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

I recently visited Fountain Square and was blown away by the variety of things to do. I started my evening at the Hi-Fi, a music venue that hosts a variety of local and national acts. From there, I walked to Thunderbird, a trendy bar that serves up delicious cocktails and small plates. I ended my night at La Margarita, a Mexican restaurant with a cozy atmosphere and amazing food. Overall, Fountain Square has something for everyone and is a must-visit neighborhood in Indianapolis.

2. Mass Ave

Mass Ave is one of the most iconic neighborhoods in Indianapolis. It's known for its historic architecture, trendy shops, and award-winning restaurants. The neighborhood is also home to a variety of theaters and performance venues, making it a popular spot for arts and culture enthusiasts.

During my visit to Mass Ave, I spent my afternoon exploring the local shops and boutiques. I found unique gifts and souvenirs at Homespun: Modern Handmade and enjoyed a delicious cup of coffee at Calvin Fletcher's Coffee Company. For dinner, I visited The Eagle, a popular restaurant that serves up Southern-style comfort food. Mass Ave is a must-visit neighborhood for anyone looking to experience the best of Indianapolis.

3. Broad Ripple

Broad Ripple is a popular neighborhood among young professionals and college students. It's known for its vibrant nightlife, with a variety of bars and clubs. The neighborhood is also home to the Monon Trail, a popular biking and walking trail that connects to downtown Indianapolis.

During my visit to Broad Ripple, I spent my evening exploring the local bars and clubs. I started at HopCat, a craft beer bar that offers over 130 beers on tap. From there, I walked to Brick House Dueling Piano Bar, a lively bar with talented musicians. I ended my night at The Red Room, a popular nightclub with a great dance floor. Broad Ripple is a must-visit neighborhood for anyone looking to experience the nightlife in Indianapolis.

4. Irvington

Irvington is a charming and historic neighborhood on the east side of Indianapolis. It's known for its beautiful homes and tree-lined streets. The neighborhood is also home to a variety of locally owned shops and restaurants.

During my visit to Irvington, I spent my afternoon exploring the local shops and restaurants. I found unique gifts at Black Sheep Gifts and enjoyed a delicious sandwich at Jockamo Upper Crust Pizza. I also visited the Irvington Halloween Festival, a popular event that celebrates the neighborhood's spooky history. Irvington is a must-visit neighborhood for anyone looking to experience the charm and history of Indianapolis.
