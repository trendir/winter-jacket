What Not To Wear In Indian Wedding A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Oct 09, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Indian weddings are a grand affair, and dressing up for them can be quite challenging. You want to look your best, but at the same time, you need to be mindful of the cultural norms and traditions. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide what to wear. However, there are certain things that you should avoid wearing to an Indian wedding. In this blog post, we will discuss what not to wear in Indian wedding and why.

Why You Should be Careful About What You Wear to an Indian Wedding

Indian weddings are steeped in tradition and culture, and dressing up for these events is an important aspect of the celebrations. The bride and the groom are usually dressed in traditional attire, and guests are expected to dress appropriately as well. However, with the changing times, people have become more experimental with their fashion choices. While it's great to be creative with your outfit, there are certain things that are considered inappropriate for an Indian wedding.

Avoid Wearing White or Black

White is considered to be the color of mourning in Indian culture, and black is associated with funerals. Therefore, it's best to avoid wearing these colors to an Indian wedding. Opt for bright colors like red, pink, yellow, or green instead. These colors symbolize happiness, prosperity, and joy, which are the essence of an Indian wedding.

Don't Wear Revealing Clothes

Indian weddings are a family affair, and it's important to dress modestly. Avoid wearing clothes that are too revealing or have plunging necklines. It's also best to avoid short dresses or skirts. Instead, opt for traditional attire like sarees, lehengas, or salwar kameez. These outfits are not only elegant but also comfortable to wear for long hours.

Avoid Wearing Denim

While denim is a versatile fabric, it's not suitable for an Indian wedding. Denim is considered to be too casual for such an occasion, and it's best to avoid wearing it altogether. Instead, opt for fabrics like silk, chiffon, or cotton, which are more appropriate for an Indian wedding.

Avoid Wearing Heavy Jewelry

While Indian weddings are known for their elaborate jewelry, it's important not to go overboard with it. Avoid wearing heavy jewelry that can weigh you down or cause discomfort. Instead, opt for minimalistic jewelry that complements your outfit. You can also experiment with statement pieces like a maang tikka or jhumkas.

Conclusion of What Not to Wear in Indian Wedding

Indian weddings are a celebration of love, happiness, and togetherness. Dressing up for such an occasion can be a daunting task, but with a little bit of effort, you can look your best. Remember to avoid wearing white or black, revealing clothes, denim, and heavy jewelry. Instead, opt for traditional attire in bright colors and minimalistic jewelry. By following these tips, you can ensure that you look and feel your best at an Indian wedding.

Question and Answer

Q. Can I Wear a Western Outfit to an Indian Wedding?

A. While it's not recommended to wear a western outfit to an Indian wedding, you can experiment with fusion wear. You can pair a western top with a traditional skirt or wear a saree with a crop top.

Q. What Kind of Footwear Should I Wear to an Indian Wedding?

A. Indian weddings involve a lot of dancing and walking, so it's important to wear comfortable footwear. You can opt for sandals, juttis, or wedges.

Q. Can I Wear Black Jeans to an Indian Wedding?

A. No, it's not recommended to wear black jeans to an Indian wedding. Instead, opt for traditional attire or formal wear.

Q. Can I Wear a Sleeveless Blouse with a Saree?

A. While it's not recommended to wear a sleeveless blouse with a saree, you can opt for a blouse with sheer sleeves or a three-quarter sleeve.
