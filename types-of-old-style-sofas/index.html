Types Of Old Style Sofas A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to add a touch of vintage charm to your living room? Old style sofas can be a great way to achieve that classic look. However, with so many types of old style sofas on the market, it can be overwhelming to decide which one would be the best fit for your home. In this guide, we'll take you through the most popular types of old style sofas and help you find the perfect one for your space.

When it comes to shopping for old style sofas, there are a few pain points that you might encounter. For one, the sheer number of options available can make it difficult to make a decision. Additionally, older sofas may not be as comfortable as modern options, which can be a dealbreaker for some buyers. Finally, the cost of these pieces can also be a concern, as they are often more expensive than newer, mass-produced options.

If you're looking for an old style sofa, it's likely that you have a specific aesthetic in mind. Perhaps you're drawn to the ornate embellishments of the Victorian era or the sleek lines of mid-century modern design. Understanding your personal style preferences can help you narrow down your options and find a piece that you'll love for years to come.

In summary, types of old style sofas can be a great addition to any living room, but it's important to consider factors such as comfort, cost, and personal style when making a purchase. Now, let's dive into some of the most popular types of old style sofas.

1. Chesterfield Sofa

The Chesterfield sofa is perhaps the most iconic old style sofa. This piece is characterized by its button-tufted back and arms, rolled arms, and nailhead trim. Originally designed in the 18th century, the Chesterfield has remained a popular choice for those seeking a classic look. I remember my grandfather's Chesterfield sofa which was passed down to my parents and still exists in our family room.

2. Camelback Sofa

The camelback sofa is another classic option that has been around since the 18th century. This sofa gets its name from its distinctive arched back, which resembles the hump of a camel. Camelback sofas often feature exposed wood frames and legs, as well as ornate carvings and detailing. My aunt has a beautiful camelback sofa in her formal living room that is over 100 years old and still looks stunning.

3. Lawson Sofa

The Lawson sofa is a more recent addition to the world of old style sofas, having been introduced in the early 20th century. This piece is characterized by its boxy shape, low back, and deep seats. Lawson sofas often have exposed wood legs and can be upholstered in a variety of fabrics. I recently purchased a Lawson sofa for my own home and love how comfortable and stylish it is.

4. Tuxedo Sofa

The tuxedo sofa is another modern take on an old style sofa. This piece is characterized by its clean lines and rectangular shape. Tuxedo sofas often have high, straight arms and a low back, giving them a sleek and sophisticated look. I recently saw a stunning tuxedo sofa in a friend's home that was upholstered in a rich velvet fabric.

Question and Answer

Q: Are old style sofas comfortable?

A: It depends on the specific piece. Some old style sofas can be quite comfortable, while others may not offer as much support as modern options. It's important to test out a sofa before purchasing it to ensure that it will meet your comfort needs.

Q: Are old style sofas expensive?

A: Generally speaking, old style sofas can be more expensive than newer, mass-produced options. However, this can vary depending on the age and condition of the piece, as well as the materials used to construct it.

Q: How do I care for an old style sofa?

A: The care instructions for an old style sofa will vary depending on the specific piece and the materials used to construct it. In general, it's important to avoid using harsh chemicals on the sofa and to clean up spills promptly to prevent staining. Regular dusting and vacuuming can also help to keep the piece looking its best.

Q: Where can I find old style sofas?

A: Old style sofas can be found at antique shops, vintage stores, and online marketplaces such as Etsy and Chairish. It's important to do your research and ensure that you're purchasing from a reputable seller who can provide information about the piece's age, condition, and history.

Conclusion of Types of Old Style Sofas

Whether you're drawn to the ornate details of a Chesterfield sofa or the sleek lines of a tuxedo sofa, there are plenty of old style options to choose from. By considering factors such as comfort, cost, and personal style, you can find the perfect piece to add a touch of vintage charm to your living room.
