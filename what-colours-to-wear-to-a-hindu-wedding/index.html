What Colours To Wear To A Hindu Wedding

Written by Daniel Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Attending a Hindu wedding can be a beautiful and exciting experience, but it can also be overwhelming when it comes to deciding what to wear. Hindu weddings are known for their bright and vibrant colours, and choosing the right outfit can be a challenge for those who are not familiar with the customs and traditions.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to attending a Hindu wedding is figuring out which colours are appropriate to wear. You want to make sure you are dressed respectfully and appropriately, while also embracing the joyous and celebratory nature of the event.

The colours you wear to a Hindu wedding depend on a few different factors, including your relationship to the bride and groom, the time of day, and the location of the wedding.

Generally, it is best to avoid wearing white, black, or red to a Hindu wedding. White is typically reserved for funerals, while black is considered to be a colour of mourning. Red is reserved for the bride, and wearing this colour can be seen as disrespectful or attention-seeking.

Choosing the Right Colours

When it comes to choosing the right colours to wear to a Hindu wedding, it is important to consider the season and time of day. Bright and bold colours are typically worn during the day, while more subdued and elegant colours are appropriate for evening weddings.

Personal experience: When I attended my first Hindu wedding, I opted for a bright pink saree with gold accents. I received many compliments on my outfit, and felt comfortable and respectful throughout the event.

For men, a traditional sherwani or kurta with a jacket is a popular choice. These outfits come in a variety of colours and styles, and can be dressed up or down depending on the formality of the event.

Accessorizing Your Outfit

Accessories are an important part of any wedding outfit, and can help to tie your look together. For women, a simple pair of gold or silver earrings and bangles can add a touch of elegance to your outfit. A small clutch or purse in a complementary colour can also be a useful addition.

Personal experience: I paired my pink saree with a pair of gold chandelier earrings and a gold clutch. These simple accessories added a touch of glamour to my outfit without overpowering it.

For men, a simple turban or pagdi can be a stylish addition to your wedding outfit. A pair of traditional mojari shoes can also complete the look.

Colour Symbolism in Hindu Weddings

Colour symbolism is an important part of Hindu weddings, and different colours hold different meanings. For example, yellow is a symbol of purity and spirituality, while green represents new beginnings and growth.

Personal experience: The bride at the Hindu wedding I attended wore a stunning green and gold saree, which symbolized her new journey with her husband.

Tips for Choosing the Right Outfit

When choosing your outfit for a Hindu wedding, it is important to consider the formality of the event, as well as the customs and traditions of the couple and their families. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

- Avoid wearing white, black, or red

- Choose bright and bold colours for daytime weddings

- Opt for more subdued and elegant colours for evening weddings

- Consider the season and time of day when choosing your outfit

- Accessorize with simple jewellery and a complementary purse or clutch

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a saree to a Hindu wedding if I am not Indian?

A: Yes, wearing a saree to a Hindu wedding is a beautiful and respectful way to embrace the culture and customs of the event.

Q: Can I wear a black suit to a Hindu wedding if it is formal?

A: It is best to avoid wearing black to a Hindu wedding, as it is considered to be a colour of mourning. Opt for a different colour suit, such as navy or grey.

Q: Can I wear a red saree to a Hindu wedding if I am not the bride?

A: It is best to avoid wearing red to a Hindu wedding, as it is reserved for the bride. Opt for a different colour saree or outfit.

Q: Can I wear jeans to a Hindu wedding?

A: It is best to avoid wearing jeans to a Hindu wedding, as it is not considered to be dressy enough for the occasion. Opt for a more formal outfit, such as a saree or a suit.

Conclusion of What Colours to Wear to a Hindu Wedding

Choosing the right colours to wear to a Hindu wedding can be a challenge, but it is an important part of showing respect and celebrating the joyous occasion. By considering the formality of the event, the customs and traditions of the couple and their families, and the symbolism of different colours, you can choose an outfit that is both beautiful and appropriate.
