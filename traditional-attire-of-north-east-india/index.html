The Mekhela Chador of Assam

My personal experience with traditional attire of North East India began when I visited Assam and saw women wearing the Mekhela Chador. The Mekhela Chador is a two-piece garment that is worn by women in Assam. The bottom piece, called the Mekhela, is a long skirt that is draped around the waist and tucked in at the back. The top piece, called the Chador, is a long piece of cloth that is draped over the head and shoulders. The Mekhela Chador is made of silk or cotton and is often decorated with intricate designs and embroidery.

The Mekhela Chador is traditionally worn on special occasions such as weddings, festivals, and other celebrations. It is a beautiful and elegant garment that is sure to turn heads wherever you go.

The Phanek and Innaphi of Manipur

Another traditional attire of North East India that is worth mentioning is the Phanek and Innaphi of Manipur. The Phanek is a long skirt that is wrapped around the waist and tucked in at the back, while the Innaphi is a long piece of cloth that is draped over the head and shoulders. The Phanek and Innaphi are made of cotton or silk and are often decorated with beautiful designs and patterns.

The Traditional Attire of Nagaland
