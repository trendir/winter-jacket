What To Wear To A Friend s Wedding As A Male

Written by Daniel Jul 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Attending a wedding can be exciting, but the thought of what to wear can be daunting. As a male, you want to look sharp and appropriate for the occasion, but you also don't want to go overboard or under-dress. It's important to strike the right balance to ensure you're not sticking out like a sore thumb.

Weddings are typically formal events, but the dress code can vary depending on the location, time of day, and theme. This can make it difficult to know what to wear, especially if you're not a fashion-forward individual. You don't want to be the one guest who shows up in jeans and a t-shirt while everyone else is dressed in suits and ties.

So, what should you wear to a friend's wedding as a male? The answer depends on a few factors, such as the dress code, season, and location. However, there are some general guidelines that can help you make the right fashion choices.

Firstly, it's important to understand the different dress codes. If the invitation specifies a dress code, follow it. If not, you can use the time of day and location as a guide. For example, a daytime wedding held outdoors calls for lighter fabrics and colors, while an evening wedding in a ballroom may require a darker suit and dress shoes. You can never go wrong with a classic suit and tie, but if you're attending a beach wedding or a more casual affair, you can opt for a blazer and slacks instead.

In summary, here are some key points to keep in mind when deciding what to wear to a friend's wedding as a male:

The Classic Suit

When in doubt, go for the classic suit and tie. A well-fitted suit in black, navy, or gray is a timeless choice that will always be appropriate for a wedding. Pair it with a crisp white dress shirt and a tie that complements the suit color. Finish off the look with dress shoes and a belt that match the color of the suit.

The Blazer and Slacks

If the dress code is more casual or the wedding is taking place in a less formal venue, you can opt for a blazer and slacks. Choose a blazer in a neutral color such as navy, gray or beige, and pair it with complementary slacks. You can add a tie or pocket square to dress it up, but it's not necessary. Finish off the look with dress shoes or loafers.

The Summer Wedding

If the wedding is taking place in the summer, you'll want to choose lighter fabrics and colors. A cotton or linen suit in a light color such as beige, light gray or khaki is a great option. Pair it with a light-colored dress shirt and tie, and finish off the look with brown or tan dress shoes.

The Beach Wedding

If the wedding is taking place on the beach, you'll want to dress for the occasion while still looking sharp. Opt for a lightweight suit in a light color such as beige or light blue. You can skip the tie and wear a dress shirt with the sleeves rolled up. Finish off the look with loafers or boat shoes.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a tuxedo to a friend's wedding?

A: Unless the wedding is a black-tie affair, a tuxedo is too formal. Stick to a classic suit or blazer and slacks.

Q: Can I wear jeans to a friend's wedding?

A: No, jeans are too casual for a wedding. Stick to dress pants or slacks.

Q: Can I wear sneakers to a friend's wedding?

A: No, sneakers are too casual for a wedding. Stick to dress shoes or loafers.

Q: Can I wear a patterned suit to a friend's wedding?

A: It depends on the pattern and the dress code. If the pattern is subtle and the dress code is not too formal, it can work. However, if the pattern is bold or the dress code is black-tie, stick to a classic suit.

Conclusion of What to Wear to a Friend's Wedding as a Male

Attending a friend's wedding can be a fun and memorable experience, but it's important to dress appropriately. By following these guidelines and considering the dress code, season, and location, you can ensure that you look sharp and stylish without stealing the spotlight from the happy couple. Remember, it's their day, not yours, so dress to impress while still being respectful of the occasion.
