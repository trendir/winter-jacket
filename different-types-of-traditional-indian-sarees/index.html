Discover The Different Types Of Traditional Indian Sarees

Written by Luffy May 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to traditional Indian attire, sarees are a timeless classic. These elegant garments have been worn by women in India for centuries and come in a variety of styles, colors, and fabrics. However, with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the perfect saree for your occasion. In this blog post, we will explore the different types of traditional Indian sarees and help you find the perfect one for your next event.

Whether you are attending a wedding, a religious ceremony, or a formal event, choosing the right saree is crucial. Different types of sarees suit different occasions, and it is essential to understand the target of each saree.

In this post, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide to the different types of traditional Indian sarees available. We will explain the target of each saree and provide you with some tips on how to style them. By the end of this post, you will be able to choose the perfect saree for your next event with confidence.

So, let's dive right in and explore the world of traditional Indian sarees.

1. Banarasi Saree

Target: Weddings, formal events

The Banarasi saree is one of the most popular types of traditional Indian sarees. It is made from pure silk and is decorated with intricate gold and silver zari work. The Banarasi saree is known for its rich, luxurious look and is often worn at weddings and formal events.

One of my favorite memories in a Banarasi saree was at my cousin's wedding. I felt like a queen in my elegant saree, and the compliments I received made me feel like a million dollars. To style a Banarasi saree, pair it with traditional jewelry and a matching clutch for a classic, timeless look.

2. Kanjeevaram Saree

Target: Weddings, religious ceremonies

The Kanjeevaram saree is another popular type of traditional Indian saree. It is made from pure silk and is known for its vibrant colors and intricate designs. The Kanjeevaram saree is often worn at weddings and religious ceremonies and is a popular choice among South Indian brides.

My sister wore a Kanjeevaram saree at her wedding, and she looked absolutely stunning. She paired it with gold jewelry and a bold red lip for a regal, elegant look. To style a Kanjeevaram saree, opt for gold jewelry and a simple, sleek hairstyle to let the saree speak for itself.

3. Chanderi Saree

Target: Day events, casual gatherings

The Chanderi saree is a lightweight, airy saree that is perfect for day events and casual gatherings. It is made from cotton or silk and is known for its sheer texture and delicate embroidery. The Chanderi saree is a popular choice among women who prefer a more understated, elegant look.

I wore a Chanderi saree to a friend's baby shower, and I received so many compliments on my outfit. I paired it with minimal jewelry and a messy bun for a relaxed, effortless look. To style a Chanderi saree, opt for delicate, simple jewelry and a natural makeup look.

4. Patola Saree

Target: Festivals, religious ceremonies

The Patola saree is a type of saree that originated in Gujarat and is known for its bold, vibrant colors and intricate designs. It is made from silk and is often worn at festivals and religious ceremonies. The Patola saree is a popular choice among women who want to make a statement with their outfit.

I wore a Patola saree at a friend's wedding, and I received so many compliments on my outfit. I paired it with statement jewelry and a bold lip for a dramatic, glamorous look. To style a Patola saree, opt for bold, colorful jewelry and a sleek, sophisticated hairstyle.

Question and Answer Section:

Q: What is the difference between a Banarasi saree and a Kanjeevaram saree?

A: The main difference between a Banarasi saree and a Kanjeevaram saree is the type of silk used to make them. Banarasi sarees are made from pure silk and are decorated with intricate zari work, while Kanjeevaram sarees are made from pure silk and are known for their vibrant colors and intricate designs.

Q: Can I wear a Chanderi saree to a formal event?

A: While Chanderi sarees are typically worn to day events and casual gatherings, they can be dressed up for a more formal occasion. To do this, opt for more elaborate jewelry and a more sophisticated hairstyle.

Q: What type of jewelry should I wear with a Patola saree?

A: Patola sarees are often worn at festivals and religious ceremonies, so it is common to pair them with traditional, bold jewelry such as jhumkas and bangles.

Q: Can I wear a Kanjeevaram saree to a non-Indian event?

A: Absolutely! Kanjeevaram sarees are known for their vibrant colors and intricate designs, making them a great choice for any event where you want to make a statement with your outfit.

Conclusion:

Traditional Indian sarees are a timeless classic that can be worn at a variety of events. From weddings to festivals to casual gatherings, there is a saree for every occasion. In this blog post, we explored the different types of traditional Indian sarees and provided tips on how to style them. Whether you prefer a regal, elegant look or a more relaxed, understated style, there is a saree out there for you. So go ahead and experiment with different types of sarees and find the perfect one for your next event!
