Types Of Indian Sarees A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Sep 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to traditional Indian attire, sarees are a staple in every woman's wardrobe. These six-yard-long pieces of cloth not only accentuate a woman's beauty but also represent Indian culture and tradition. However, with so many different types of Indian sarees available in the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the one that suits your style and occasion. In this article, we will explore the various types of Indian sarees and help you make an informed decision.

Choosing the perfect saree can be a daunting task, especially if you are new to the world of Indian fashion. Factors such as the occasion, fabric, design, and draping style can leave you confused and overwhelmed. But worry not, we are here to guide you through the process and help you choose the best saree that fits your needs.

What are the different types of Indian sarees?

Indian sarees come in a wide range of fabrics, designs, and draping styles. Each type of saree is unique and has its own charm. Let's take a look at some of the popular types of Indian sarees:

Banarasi Sarees

Banarasi sarees are one of the most popular types of Indian sarees. These sarees originated from the city of Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh and are known for their intricate designs and heavy embroidery work. Banarasi sarees are made of silk and are perfect for weddings and other formal occasions.

Kanjivaram Sarees

Kanjivaram sarees, also known as Kanchipuram sarees, are made in the town of Kanchipuram in Tamil Nadu. These sarees are made of pure silk and are known for their rich texture and bright colors. Kanjivaram sarees are perfect for weddings and other special occasions.

Chanderi Sarees

Chanderi sarees are made in the town of Chanderi in Madhya Pradesh. These sarees are made of lightweight cotton or silk and have a sheer texture. Chanderi sarees are known for their subtle designs and are perfect for everyday wear.

Bandhani Sarees

Bandhani sarees, also known as tie-and-dye sarees, are made by tying small portions of cloth with a thread and then dyeing it. These sarees are popular in Gujarat and Rajasthan and are known for their vibrant colors and intricate designs.

Paithani Sarees

Paithani sarees are made in the city of Paithan in Maharashtra. These sarees are made of pure silk and are known for their intricate designs and bright colors. Paithani sarees are perfect for weddings and other formal occasions.

My Personal Experience with Indian Sarees

As an Indian woman, sarees have always been a part of my life. I have fond memories of my mother and grandmother draping sarees for weddings and other special occasions. When I got married, I wore a traditional Kanjivaram saree for my wedding reception. The saree was made of pure silk and had intricate designs in gold and red. It was a perfect blend of tradition and modernity.

Since then, I have collected a few more sarees, including a Banarasi saree and a Chanderi saree. I love how each saree tells a story and represents a different part of India's rich culture and heritage.

How to Choose the Right Indian Saree?

Choosing the right saree can be a daunting task, but it can also be a fun and exciting experience. Here are a few tips to help you choose the right Indian saree:

1. Consider the Occasion

The occasion plays a crucial role in determining the type of saree you should wear. For weddings and other formal occasions, opt for a Banarasi or Kanjivaram saree. For everyday wear, choose a Chanderi or cotton saree.

2. Choose the Right Fabric

The fabric of the saree can make or break your look. Choose a fabric that suits your body type and the occasion. Silk sarees are perfect for weddings and other formal occasions, while cotton sarees are great for everyday wear.

3. Consider the Design and Color

The design and color of the saree should complement your skin tone and body type. Choose a color that highlights your best features and a design that accentuates your curves.

Conclusion of Types of Indian Sarees

Indian sarees are more than just a piece of clothing; they represent the rich cultural heritage of India. With so many different types of Indian sarees available, there is a saree for every occasion and style. By considering the occasion, fabric, design, and draping style, you can choose the perfect saree that suits your needs and personality.

Question and Answer

Q. What is the most popular type of Indian saree?

A. Banarasi sarees are one of the most popular types of Indian sarees. These sarees are known for their intricate designs and heavy embroidery work.

Q. Can I wear a saree for everyday wear?

A. Yes, you can wear a saree for everyday wear. Cotton sarees and Chanderi sarees are perfect for everyday wear.

Q. What is the difference between a Banarasi saree and a Kanjivaram saree?

A. Banarasi sarees are made in the city of Varanasi and are known for their intricate designs and heavy embroidery work. Kanjivaram sarees, on the other hand, are made in Kanchipuram and are known for their rich texture and bright colors.

Q. How do I drape a saree?

A. There are many different ways to drape a saree, depending on the fabric and occasion. You can learn how to drape a saree from YouTube tutorials or by taking lessons from a professional.
