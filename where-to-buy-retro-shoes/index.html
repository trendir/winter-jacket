Where To Buy Retro Shoes Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you a fan of vintage fashion? Do you love the look and feel of retro shoes? If so, you may be wondering where to buy retro shoes. Finding the perfect pair of vintage sneakers or pumps can be a challenge, especially if you're not sure where to start. But don't worry - we've got you covered. In this post, we'll explore the best places to buy retro shoes, as well as some tips for finding the perfect pair.

If you're a fan of retro shoes, you may have experienced some frustrations when it comes to finding them. Maybe you've scoured your local thrift stores and vintage shops without success. Or perhaps you've found a pair online, only to discover that they're not the right size or style. It can be frustrating to find the perfect pair of retro shoes, but don't give up hope. There are plenty of great places to buy retro shoes, both online and offline.

So, where can you buy retro shoes? Here are a few options:

Online Vintage Shops

One of the best places to buy retro shoes is online vintage shops. There are plenty of online retailers that specialize in vintage clothing and accessories, including shoes. Some popular options include Etsy, Beyond Retro, and Rokit. When buying from an online vintage shop, be sure to check the seller's ratings and read reviews from previous customers. This can help you avoid scams and ensure that you're getting a high-quality product.

Vintage Fairs and Markets

If you prefer to shop in person, vintage fairs and markets are a great option. These events are often held in major cities and offer a wide range of vintage clothing and accessories, including retro shoes. You can browse multiple vendors in one location, which can save you time and help you find the perfect pair of shoes. Some popular vintage fairs include the Brooklyn Flea in New York City and the Rose Bowl Flea Market in Los Angeles.

Thrift Stores and Charity Shops

Another option for finding retro shoes is thrift stores and charity shops. These shops often have a rotating selection of vintage clothing and accessories, including shoes. While you may need to do some digging, you can often find unique and affordable retro shoes at thrift stores. Some popular thrift store chains include Goodwill and The Salvation Army.

Specialty Shoe Stores

Finally, you can also find retro shoes at specialty shoe stores. Some shoe stores specialize in vintage or retro-style footwear, making it easier to find the perfect pair. Some popular options include ModCloth and Rocket Originals. While these stores may be more expensive than thrift stores or online vintage shops, they often offer a wider selection and higher-quality products.

Now that you know where to buy retro shoes, let's recap. You can find retro shoes at online vintage shops, vintage fairs and markets, thrift stores and charity shops, and specialty shoe stores. Each option has its own pros and cons, so be sure to weigh your options and choose the one that's best for you.

Personal Experience: Buying Retro Shoes Online

Personally, I've had the most success finding retro shoes online. I've purchased several pairs from Etsy and have always been happy with my purchases. When buying online, I always check the seller's ratings and read reviews from previous customers. This helps me feel confident that I'm getting a high-quality product.

Personal Experience: Finding Retro Shoes at Vintage Fairs

I've also had luck finding retro shoes at vintage fairs and markets. I attended the Brooklyn Flea in New York City and was able to find a pair of vintage pumps that were the perfect fit. It was great to be able to try on multiple pairs from different vendors in one location.

The Benefits of Buying Retro Shoes

Now that you know where to buy retro shoes, you may be wondering why you should bother. After all, retro shoes can be more expensive than modern shoes, and they may not be as comfortable or durable. However, there are plenty of benefits to buying retro shoes.

For one, retro shoes are often more unique than modern shoes. You're less likely to see someone else wearing the same pair of shoes as you, which can be a fun way to express your individuality. Retro shoes also have a sense of history and nostalgia that modern shoes can't match. Finally, retro shoes are often made from higher-quality materials and are more durable than modern shoes. While you may need to break them in, they'll last for years to come.

Tips for Buying Retro Shoes

If you're new to buying retro shoes, there are a few tips to keep in mind. First, pay attention to sizing. Vintage shoes may be sized differently than modern shoes, so be sure to measure your feet and compare them to the seller's sizing chart. Second, be prepared to break in your shoes. Retro shoes may be stiffer and less comfortable than modern shoes at first, but they'll become more comfortable over time. Finally, be patient. Finding the perfect pair of retro shoes may take some time and effort, but it's worth it in the end.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I find retro shoes at my local shoe store?

A: It depends on the store. Some specialty shoe stores may carry retro-style footwear, but most mainstream shoe stores do not.

Q: How do I know if a pair of retro shoes is authentic?

A: This can be difficult to determine, especially if you're buying online. Look for sellers with good ratings and reviews and ask for additional photos or information if necessary. You can also research the brand and style of the shoe to get a better idea of what to look for.

Q: Are retro shoes comfortable to wear?

A: Retro shoes may be stiffer and less comfortable than modern shoes, especially at first. However, they will become more comfortable over time as you break them in.

Q: How much should I expect to pay for retro shoes?

A: The price of retro shoes can vary widely depending on the brand, style, and condition of the shoe. You can find affordable options at thrift stores and vintage fairs, while specialty stores and online shops may be more expensive.

Conclusion of Where to Buy Retro Shoes

Overall, there are plenty of great places to buy retro shoes, both online and offline. Whether you prefer to shop in person or online, there's sure to be a pair of vintage sneakers or pumps that are perfect for you. Just be sure to pay attention to sizing, break in your shoes, and be patient in your search. Happy shoe shopping!
