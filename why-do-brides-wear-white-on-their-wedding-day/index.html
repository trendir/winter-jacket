Why Do Brides Wear White On Their Wedding Day

Written by Aprilia Oct 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Wedding days are one of the most important days in a person's life, and brides often find themselves in a dilemma when it comes to choosing their wedding dress. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide on the perfect dress. However, one tradition that has been around for centuries is the bride wearing a white dress. But why do brides wear white on their wedding day?

Many brides may not even think about the reason behind wearing a white dress on their wedding day. However, understanding the significance of this tradition can add to the special meaning of the day.

The tradition of wearing a white dress on one's wedding day dates back to the Victorian era. Queen Victoria started the trend in 1840 when she wore a white dress to her wedding. Before this, brides would wear dresses of different colors, and white was not the most popular choice. However, after Queen Victoria's wedding, white became the color of choice for brides and symbolized purity, innocence, and a new beginning.

In summary, brides wear white on their wedding day because it symbolizes purity, innocence, and a new beginning. This tradition dates back to the Victorian era when Queen Victoria wore a white dress to her wedding, and it has remained popular ever since.

But Why is Purity So Important?

Personally, I remember growing up and hearing my mother and grandmother talk about the importance of purity on a woman's wedding day. They believed that a bride should be pure and innocent, and wearing a white dress symbolized this. However, this belief has shifted over time, and nowadays, brides wear white simply because it is a tradition.

The Modern Take on Wearing White

Although the tradition of wearing white has remained popular, many brides are choosing to break away from this tradition and opt for dresses of different colors. Some brides prefer to wear colors that complement their skin tone or the theme of their wedding. Others choose to wear dresses that have sentimental value, such as their mother's or grandmother's wedding dress.

The Symbolism of a White Dress

Despite the shift towards more colorful dresses, the symbolism behind wearing a white dress remains the same. The white dress symbolizes a fresh start, a new beginning, and a pure heart. It is a statement of the bride's commitment to her partner and the start of their journey together.

How to Choose the Perfect Dress

When choosing a wedding dress, it is important to consider the symbolism behind the color. If you want to follow tradition and wear a white dress, consider the style and fit that best suits your body type. If you prefer to break away from tradition and opt for a different color, choose a color that complements your skin tone and the theme of your wedding. Above all, choose a dress that makes you feel beautiful and confident on your special day.

My Personal Experience

On my wedding day, I chose to wear a white dress that was simple and elegant. Although I did not give much thought to the symbolism behind the color, I felt beautiful and confident in my dress. Looking back, I am glad that I chose to follow tradition and wear a white dress.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can brides wear a different color on their wedding day?

A: Yes, brides can wear dresses of different colors on their wedding day. However, white remains the most popular choice and symbolizes purity and a new beginning.

Q: What is the significance of a white dress?

A: A white dress symbolizes purity, innocence, and a new beginning.

Q: Why did Queen Victoria wear a white dress to her wedding?

A: Queen Victoria wore a white dress to her wedding in 1840, and this started the trend of wearing white dresses on one's wedding day. Before this, brides would wear dresses of different colors.

Q: Can a bride wear her mother's wedding dress?

A: Yes, a bride can wear her mother's or grandmother's wedding dress if it has sentimental value. This is a great way to honor family traditions and add a personal touch to one's wedding day.

Conclusion

The tradition of wearing a white dress on one's wedding day has been around for centuries and symbolizes purity, innocence, and a new beginning. Although many brides are opting to break away from this tradition and wear dresses of different colors, the symbolism remains the same. When choosing a wedding dress, it is important to consider the symbolism behind the color and choose a dress that makes you feel beautiful and confident on your special day.
