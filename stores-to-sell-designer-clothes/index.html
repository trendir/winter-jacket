Where To Sell Designer Clothes A Guide To Finding The Best Stores

Written by Oliver May 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to sell designer clothes but don't know where to start? Do you want to make sure you're getting the best price for your high-end items? If so, you're in the right place. In this guide, we'll take a look at the best stores to sell designer clothes and provide you with valuable tips to make the process as smooth as possible.

What is the Target of Stores to Sell Designer Clothes?

The target of stores to sell designer clothes is to provide a platform for individuals to sell their gently used or new designer clothing items. These stores typically offer a curated selection of luxury items to their customers, which means that the clothes you're looking to sell must meet a certain standard of quality.

When selling designer clothes, it's important to keep in mind that these items can be quite expensive. As a result, it's essential to find a store that will offer you a fair price for your items. Additionally, you want to make sure that the store you choose has a good reputation and a loyal customer base. This will increase the likelihood that your items will sell quickly.

The Best Stores to Sell Designer Clothes

Now that you know what to look for in a store that sells designer clothes, let's take a look at some of the best options available:

The RealReal

The RealReal is an online consignment store that specializes in luxury clothing, accessories, and home decor. They offer free in-home pickups for qualifying items and provide a hassle-free selling process. The RealReal takes a commission on all sales, but they offer competitive rates and provide sellers with the opportunity to earn more if they choose to use their earnings as store credit.

Vestiaire Collective

Vestiaire Collective is another online consignment store that focuses on luxury fashion. They offer a seamless selling process and provide sellers with the option to set their prices or have them recommended by the site. Vestiaire Collective takes a commission on all sales, but they offer competitive rates and provide sellers with a prepaid shipping label for their items.

Tradesy

Tradesy is an online marketplace that allows individuals to sell their designer clothes and accessories. They offer a simple selling process and provide sellers with a prepaid shipping label for their items. Tradesy takes a commission on all sales, but they offer competitive rates and provide sellers with the option to keep their earnings in their Tradesy account or transfer them to their bank account.

How to Maximize Your Profits

While the stores we've mentioned above all offer competitive rates, there are a few things you can do to maximize your profits when selling designer clothes:

  • Take high-quality photos of your items
  • Provide detailed descriptions of your items, including any flaws or imperfections
  • Price your items competitively
  • Consider offering discounts or promotions to encourage sales

My Personal Experience Selling Designer Clothes

As someone who has sold designer clothes in the past, I can attest to the importance of choosing the right store. I've had the most success selling my items on The RealReal, as they offer a seamless selling process and competitive rates. However, I've also had success selling items on Vestiaire Collective and Tradesy.

When selling designer clothes, it's important to be patient and to take the time to properly price and describe your items. Additionally, it's important to keep in mind that you may not get the full retail value for your items, but selling them can still be a great way to earn some extra cash.

Question and Answer

What designer brands do these stores accept?

The RealReal, Vestiaire Collective, and Tradesy all accept a wide range of designer brands, including but not limited to Chanel, Gucci, Prada, and Louis Vuitton.

What percentage of the sale do these stores take?

The RealReal and Tradesy take a commission of around 20-30% on all sales, while Vestiaire Collective takes a commission of around 15-25% depending on the sale price.

Do I need to have the original packaging and tags to sell my items?

No, you do not need to have the original packaging and tags to sell your items. However, it's important to accurately describe the condition of your items and provide detailed photos to ensure a smooth selling process.

Can I sell items that are not in perfect condition?

Yes, you can still sell items that have minor flaws or imperfections. However, it's important to accurately describe the condition of your items and provide detailed photos to ensure that buyers are aware of any flaws before making a purchase.

Conclusion of Stores to Sell Designer Clothes

When it comes to selling designer clothes, it's important to choose a store that offers a fair price and a seamless selling process. The RealReal, Vestiaire Collective, and Tradesy are all great options to consider, but it's important to take the time to properly price and describe your items to maximize your profits. With these tips in mind, you'll be well on your way to selling your designer clothes for top dollar.
