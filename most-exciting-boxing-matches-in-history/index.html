The Thrilla in Manila: Muhammad Ali vs. Joe Frazier III

The Thrilla in Manila is widely considered to be one of the greatest boxing matches of all time. The fight took place on October 1, 1975, and featured two of the greatest boxers of all time: Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. Ali and Frazier had already fought twice before, with each fighter winning one match. The Thrilla in Manila was the rubber match, and it did not disappoint.

The fight was incredibly intense from start to finish, with both fighters landing blow after blow. Ali eventually emerged victorious in the 14th round, but both fighters had given everything they had in the ring. The Thrilla in Manila is a testament to the incredible skill and determination of both Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier.

The Rumble in the Jungle: Muhammad Ali vs. George Foreman

The Rumble in the Jungle is another one of the most exciting boxing matches in history. The fight took place on October 30, 1974, and featured Muhammad Ali and George Foreman. Foreman was the heavy favorite going into the fight, but Ali was able to use his incredible footwork and quickness to wear Foreman down.

Ali eventually won the fight in the eighth round, and the Rumble in the Jungle became an instant classic. The fight is remembered not only for Ali's incredible victory but also for the hype and excitement that surrounded the event.

The Fight of the Century: Muhammad Ali vs. Joe Frazier I

The Fight of the Century was the first fight between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier, and it took place on March 8, 1971. The fight was hyped as the biggest boxing match of all time, and it did not disappoint. Both fighters were at the top of their game, and the fight was incredibly intense from start to finish.

In the end, Joe Frazier emerged victorious, but the Fight of the Century is remembered as one of the greatest boxing matches of all time.

The Fight: Sugar Ray Leonard vs. Thomas Hearns

The Fight between Sugar Ray Leonard and Thomas Hearns took place on September 16, 1981, and it was an instant classic. Both fighters were undefeated going into the fight, and they put on a show for the ages. The fight was incredibly close, with both fighters landing some devastating blows.

In the end, Sugar Ray Leonard emerged victorious in the 14th round, but the fight is remembered as one of the greatest in boxing history.
