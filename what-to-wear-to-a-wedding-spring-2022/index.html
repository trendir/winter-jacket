What To Wear To A Wedding Spring 2022

Written by Oliver Oct 28, 2023 · 6 min read

Wedding season is just around the corner, and with it comes the eternal question of what to wear. You want to look stylish and put-together, but also appropriate for the occasion. With so many different dress codes and styles to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide on the perfect outfit. In this post, we'll guide you through the latest trends and provide tips on what to wear to a wedding spring 2022.

Attending a wedding can be stressful enough without the added pressure of finding the right outfit. You want to feel comfortable and confident, but also avoid any fashion faux pas. Plus, with the ongoing pandemic, there may be additional considerations such as outdoor vs. indoor events, dress codes that allow for more casual attire, and more.

First and foremost, it's important to check the invitation for any dress code guidelines. If the invitation specifies "black tie," for example, you'll want to opt for a formal gown or tuxedo. If the dress code is "cocktail attire," a knee-length dress or suit and tie would be appropriate. If there's no dress code stated, you can take cues from the location and time of day. A beach wedding in the afternoon might call for a flowy sundress, while an evening ceremony in a ballroom might require a more formal outfit.

When it comes to trends for spring 2022, pastel colors and floral prints are always a safe bet. You can't go wrong with a flowy maxi dress or a chic pantsuit in a soft pink or lavender hue. If you're feeling bold, try a bright yellow or orange dress to really make a statement. Crop tops and high-waisted skirts are also having a moment, but make sure to keep it classy by choosing a midi length skirt and a top with a higher neckline.

The Power of Accessories

Accessories can make or break an outfit, and they're especially important when it comes to wedding attire. A statement necklace, earrings, or bracelet can elevate a simple dress, while a clutch or small handbag adds a touch of elegance. For shoes, opt for comfortable yet stylish heels or dressy flats. If you're attending an outdoor wedding or the reception will be held on grass, consider wearing wedges or block heels to avoid sinking into the ground.

Dressing for the Venue

The venue of the wedding can also play a role in what to wear. If it's a beach wedding, you'll want to choose airy fabrics and open-toe shoes. For a rustic barn wedding, a flowy maxi dress or a skirt and blouse combo would be perfect. If the wedding is held in a ballroom or hotel, a formal gown or a sleek suit and tie would be appropriate. It's always better to be slightly overdressed than underdressed, so don't be afraid to dress up a bit.

Choosing the Right Fabrics

When it comes to spring weddings, lighter fabrics are key. Opt for breathable materials such as cotton or linen to stay cool in warmer weather. Avoid heavy fabrics like wool or velvet, which are better suited for fall or winter weddings. If you're attending an outdoor wedding or reception, consider bringing a light jacket or shawl in case the temperature drops in the evening.

Hair and Makeup Tips

A polished hair and makeup look can really tie your wedding outfit together. For a daytime wedding, keep your makeup light and natural with a bold lip or eye look for a pop of color. For an evening wedding, you can go for a more dramatic makeup look with smoky eyes or a bold lip. As for hair, a simple updo or loose curls are always a classic choice. If you're attending an outdoor wedding, consider a braided hairstyle to keep your hair off your face and avoid windblown tresses.

Personal Experience: My Best Wedding Outfit

For my cousin's outdoor wedding last spring, I opted for a flowy midi dress in a pastel green hue. I paired it with nude block heels and a small clutch. To complete the look, I added a statement necklace and simple stud earrings. The dress was comfortable enough to dance in all night, but still looked dressy enough for the occasion. I received so many compliments on the outfit, and felt confident and stylish all night long.

Personal Experience: My Worst Wedding Outfit

For a friend's wedding a few years ago, I made the mistake of wearing a dress that was too short and too tight. I didn't realize how short it was until I saw the photos later on - not a good look! Plus, the tightness made it difficult to dance and move around comfortably. Lesson learned: always make sure to try on your outfit beforehand and make sure it's comfortable and appropriate for the occasion.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear white to a wedding?
A: It's generally best to avoid wearing white to a wedding, as this is traditionally reserved for the bride. However, if the invitation specifies an all-white dress code or if the bride gives you permission, you can consider wearing a white outfit.

Q: Can I wear jeans to a wedding?
A: It's generally best to avoid wearing jeans to a wedding, as this is too casual for the occasion. However, if the invitation specifies a more casual dress code or if the wedding is outdoors or on a farm, you can consider wearing dressy jeans or denim trousers.

Q: Can I wear black to a wedding?
A: Yes, black is generally acceptable to wear to a wedding - just make sure to pair it with bright and cheerful accessories to avoid looking too somber.

Q: Can I wear flip flops to a wedding?
A: It's generally best to avoid wearing flip flops to a wedding, as this is too casual for the occasion. However, if the wedding is on the beach or if the invitation specifies a more casual dress code, you can consider wearing dressy sandals or espadrilles.

Conclusion of What to Wear to a Wedding Spring 2022

Attending a wedding can be a fun and exciting event, but it's important to make sure you're dressed appropriately for the occasion. By following these tips and trends for what to wear to a wedding spring 2022, you're sure to look stylish and put-together while still respecting the dress code and venue. Remember to always check the invitation for any guidelines, choose breathable fabrics, and accessorize wisely. Happy wedding season!
