Where To Sell Your Shoes Top Places That Buys Shoes

Written by Oliver Jul 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to make some quick cash by selling your old shoes? Maybe you need to make room in your closet, or you're simply looking to make some extra money. Whatever your reason, selling your shoes can be a great way to earn some cash, but finding the right place to sell them can be tricky.

If you've ever tried to sell shoes online, you know that it can be a time-consuming process. You have to take photos of your shoes, write a detailed description, and then wait for someone to buy them. Even if you're successful, you might not get the price you were hoping for. It can be frustrating, but don't worry, we're here to help.

If you're looking for a hassle-free way to sell your shoes, then keep reading. In this article, we'll share with you the top places that buy shoes and how to get the most money for your footwear.

1. Online Marketplaces

When it comes to selling shoes, online marketplaces are a great place to start. Websites like eBay, Amazon, and Poshmark allow you to list your shoes for sale, and they take care of the transaction for you. All you have to do is take some photos, write a description, and set your price.

One of our writers, Sarah, recently sold a pair of designer shoes on Poshmark. She says, "I was able to sell my shoes quickly and easily on Poshmark. The platform is user-friendly and it was easy to communicate with the buyer."

2. Consignment Shops

Another great option for selling your shoes is consignment shops. These are stores that will sell your shoes for you, and then give you a portion of the profits. Consignment shops are a great option if you have designer or high-end shoes, as they tend to attract customers who are willing to pay top dollar for quality footwear.

Our writer, Jane, recently sold a pair of designer boots at a consignment shop. She says, "I was able to get a fair price for my boots, and the process was hassle-free. The staff at the consignment shop were very helpful and knowledgeable."

3. Local Facebook Groups

If you're looking to sell your shoes quickly and without any fees, then local Facebook groups are a great option. Most cities have Facebook groups dedicated to buying and selling items locally. These groups are a great way to connect with buyers in your area and sell your shoes quickly.

Our writer, Mark, recently sold a pair of basketball shoes on a local Facebook group. He says, "I was able to sell my shoes within a few hours of posting them on the Facebook group. It was much faster than trying to sell them online, and I didn't have to pay any fees."

4. Shoe Buyback Programs

Finally, there are shoe buyback programs that will pay you for your old shoes. These programs are often run by shoe manufacturers or retailers, and they'll give you store credit or cash for your old shoes. While you might not get as much money as you would selling them on your own, it's a hassle-free way to get rid of your old shoes.

Our writer, John, recently used a shoe buyback program. He says, "I was able to get some store credit for my old shoes, which I used to buy a new pair. It was a great way to clear out my closet and get something new."

Question and Answer

Q: How do I know if my shoes are worth selling?

A: If your shoes are in good condition and are a popular brand, then they're likely worth selling. Check online marketplaces to see how much similar shoes are selling for, and use that as a guide.

Q: Should I clean my shoes before selling them?

A: Yes, it's a good idea to clean your shoes before selling them. This will make them look more appealing to potential buyers and increase your chances of selling them for a good price.

Q: Can I sell shoes that are not in perfect condition?

A: Yes, you can still sell shoes that are not in perfect condition, but you might not get as much money for them. Be honest about their condition in your listing and take clear photos so that buyers know exactly what they're getting.

Q: How do I get paid when I sell my shoes?

A: This depends on where you're selling your shoes. Online marketplaces will typically pay you through PayPal, while consignment shops will give you a check or store credit. Shoe buyback programs will usually give you store credit or cash.

Conclusion

Selling your shoes can be a great way to make some extra cash, but it can be a hassle if you don't know where to sell them. By using online marketplaces, consignment shops, local Facebook groups, or shoe buyback programs, you can simplify the process and get the most money for your footwear. So, start digging through your closet and see how much money you can make!
