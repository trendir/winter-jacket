What to Wear to an Indian Wedding as a Male Guest

Attending an Indian wedding as a male guest can be daunting, especially when it comes to the dress code. With so many cultural and traditional elements to consider, it can be overwhelming to decide what to wear. But fear not, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll guide you on what to wear to an Indian wedding as a male guest, so you can feel confident and comfortable.

The dress code for an Indian wedding can vary depending on the time of day, location, and level of formality. However, generally speaking, the dress code for male guests is a traditional Indian outfit or formal Western attire.

If you opt for a traditional Indian outfit, consider a sherwani, which is a long coat-like garment worn over a kurta and churidar pants. You can also go for a bandhgala suit, which is a formal suit with a high collar. Both options can be accessorized with a turban, mojari shoes, and a stole.

For formal Western attire, a well-tailored suit in a dark color such as black, navy or charcoal is appropriate. Pair it with a dress shirt, tie, and leather dress shoes. Avoid wearing white or bright colors, as they are typically reserved for the groom and groomsmen.

Personal Experience

As a male guest attending an Indian wedding, I opted for a traditional sherwani outfit. I chose a navy blue sherwani with gold embroidery, paired with cream churidar pants and matching mojari shoes. I accessorized with a turban and a cream stole, which completed the look. I felt comfortable and confident in my outfit, and received many compliments throughout the wedding.

Accessorizing

Accessories play a big role in completing your outfit for an Indian wedding. Consider adding a watch, cufflinks, a pocket square, and a brooch to your Western attire. For traditional Indian attire, a turban, stole, mojari shoes, and a statement piece of jewelry such as a necklace or bracelet are great options. Just remember to keep it tasteful and avoid over-accessorizing.

Cultural Considerations

It's important to be respectful of the cultural and traditional elements when choosing your outfit. Avoid wearing shorts, tank tops, and anything that exposes too much skin. Also, avoid wearing clothing with religious or political symbols, as it can be seen as disrespectful. Instead, opt for conservative and tasteful attire.

Dress Code for Different Events

The dress code for an Indian wedding can vary depending on the events. For the Sangeet or Mehendi ceremony, which is usually held the night before the wedding, you can opt for a more casual outfit such as a kurta and jeans or chinos. For the wedding ceremony itself, a traditional Indian outfit or formal Western attire is appropriate. For the reception, you can choose to switch up your outfit or stick with what you wore for the wedding ceremony.

Personal Experience

For the Sangeet ceremony, I opted for a casual yet traditional look. I wore a beige kurta with white chinos and brown loafers. I accessorized with a watch and a bracelet, which added a touch of personality to my outfit. I felt comfortable and stylish, and was able to dance the night away.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a tuxedo to an Indian wedding?

A: While a tuxedo is a formal option, it's not traditionally worn to an Indian wedding. It's better to opt for a well-tailored suit in a dark color.

Q: Can I wear a hat to an Indian wedding?

A: It's best to avoid wearing hats to an Indian wedding, as it's not a traditional accessory and can be seen as disrespectful.

Q: Can I wear sandals to an Indian wedding?

A: While sandals can be comfortable, it's better to opt for mojari shoes or dress shoes for a more formal and traditional look.

Q: Can I wear a white outfit to an Indian wedding?

A: White is typically reserved for the groom and groomsmen, so it's best to avoid wearing white to an Indian wedding.

Conclusion of What to Wear to an Indian Wedding as a Male Guest

Attending an Indian wedding as a male guest can be a fun and memorable experience, as long as you choose the appropriate attire. Whether you opt for a traditional Indian outfit or formal Western attire, make sure to consider the cultural and traditional elements when choosing your outfit. With these tips and guidelines, you'll be sure to turn heads and make a lasting impression.
