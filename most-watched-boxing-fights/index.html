The Rules of Boxing

Before watching the most watched boxing fights, it's important to understand the rules of boxing. Boxing is a combat sport in which two fighters wearing gloves punch each other in a boxing ring. The goal is to knock out the opponent or score more points by landing punches on their body or head.

Each round typically lasts three minutes, and there are usually 10-12 rounds in a professional boxing match. If a fighter is knocked down and unable to get up within 10 seconds, the fight is over. If both fighters make it to the end of the final round, the winner is determined by a panel of judges who score the fight based on the number of punches landed, the quality of punches, and overall performance.
