Why Is Red Worn At Indian Weddings

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant and colorful celebrations, and one color that stands out is red. Why is red such an important color at Indian weddings? Let's explore.

Red is a color that is deeply rooted in Indian culture and tradition. It is considered to be a symbol of love, passion, fertility, and purity. Red is also believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits.

At Indian weddings, the bride typically wears a red outfit, which is known as a "lehenga" or a "saree." The groom may also wear a red turban or a red scarf. Red is also used extensively in the wedding decorations, including the flowers, the stage, and the mandap (the canopy under which the wedding ceremony takes place).

In summary, red is worn at Indian weddings because it is considered to be a symbol of love, passion, fertility, and purity. It is also believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits. Red is used extensively in the bride's outfit, the groom's accessories, and the wedding decorations.

Red and the Bridal Outfit

As mentioned earlier, the bride typically wears a red outfit at an Indian wedding. This tradition dates back to ancient times when red was the color associated with the goddess of fertility and love, Parvati. It was believed that wearing red would bring happiness, good luck, and a long-lasting marriage to the bride.

My cousin, who got married last year, also wore a beautiful red lehenga on her wedding day. She looked stunning, and everyone admired her outfit. She later told me that she chose red because it was a traditional color, and she wanted to honor her culture and heritage.

The Significance of Red in Hinduism

In Hinduism, red is a sacred color that symbolizes purity, power, and energy. It is also associated with the goddesses Durga and Kali, who are considered to be powerful and fierce. Red is used extensively in Hindu rituals and ceremonies, including weddings.

During the wedding ceremony, the bride and groom take seven vows or promises to each other, which are known as the "Saptapadi." Each vow is accompanied by the tying of a knot in the bride's saree or lehenga, and the groom's scarf or shawl. The color of the thread used to tie the knots is usually red, symbolizing the couple's eternal bond and commitment to each other.

The Psychology of Red

Red is also known to have a psychological impact on people. It is a color that evokes strong emotions and feelings of passion, love, and desire. It is also associated with energy, power, and excitement. These qualities make red a perfect color for celebrations like weddings.

How to Incorporate Red into Your Wedding

If you are planning an Indian wedding and would like to incorporate red into your wedding, there are many ways to do so. You can choose a red lehenga or saree for the bride, a red turban or scarf for the groom, and red flowers and decorations for the wedding venue. You can also use red as an accent color in your wedding invitations, wedding favors, and other details.
