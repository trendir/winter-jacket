David Hicks

David Hicks was a British interior designer known for his bold use of color, pattern, and texture. He was also one of the first designers to mix contemporary and traditional styles, creating a look that was both timeless and modern. Hicks’ clients included royalty, celebrities, and wealthy socialites, and his work was featured in numerous magazines and design books.

Personally, I am a huge fan of David Hicks’ work. I love the way he seamlessly blends different design styles and creates spaces that feel both luxurious and approachable. One of my favorite projects of his is the interior of the Queen Mother’s house in Scotland, which features bold, geometric patterns and a playful use of color.

Billy Baldwin

Billy Baldwin was an American interior designer known for his elegant, understated style. He was a master of proportion and scale, and his designs often featured clean lines, neutral colors, and carefully curated accessories. Baldwin’s clients included the Kennedys, Babe Paley, and Cole Porter, and his work was featured in top publications such as Vogue and House Beautiful.

Billy Baldwin is one of my all-time favorite designers. I love the way he creates such serene, calming spaces that are still full of personality. One of my favorite projects of his is the living room he designed for Cole Porter’s Waldorf Astoria apartment. The room is a masterclass in understated elegance, with its muted color scheme and carefully selected furnishings.

Thom Filicia

Thom Filicia is a contemporary American interior designer known for his eclectic, playful style. He rose to fame as one of the stars of the hit TV show Queer Eye for the Straight Guy, where he showcased his talents for making over spaces on a budget. Filicia’s clients include Tina Fey, Jennifer Lopez, and Delta Airlines, and his work has been featured in Architectural Digest and Elle Decor.

I’ve always admired Thom Filicia’s ability to inject so much personality and fun into his designs. He has a real talent for mixing patterns, colors, and textures in unexpected ways, and his spaces always feel joyful and welcoming. One of my favorite projects of his is the lake house he designed for Tina Fey, which features bright hues, quirky accessories, and plenty of cozy seating areas.
